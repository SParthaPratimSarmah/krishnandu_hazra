Research

My Research Interests are:

UAV Communication and Networks

Unmanned Aerial Vehicles (UAVs) have emerged as a transformative solution in modern wireless communication networks due to their high mobility, flexibility, and rapid deployment capabilities. Unlike traditional terrestrial infrastructure, UAVs can be dynamically positioned in three-dimensional space, enabling efficient on-demand connectivity in challenging and infrastructure-limited environments such as disaster-affected areas, remote regions, and dense urban scenarios. UAV-assisted communication systems are widely applied in disaster management, emergency response, rural connectivity, precision agriculture, surveillance, environmental monitoring, and smart city ecosystems. In these systems, UAVs function as aerial base stations, relays, or data collectors, forming adaptive and self-organizing networks to support ground users. Their ability to establish line-of-sight (LoS) communication significantly improves signal quality and coverage compared to conventional ground-based systems. Recent advancements in this field are driven by the integration of emerging technologies such as 5G/6G networks, Internet of Things (IoT), edge computing, and Artificial Intelligence (AI). AI-driven optimization techniques, including machine learning and reinforcement learning, are increasingly being used to determine optimal UAV placement, trajectory planning, resource allocation, and user association. These intelligent approaches help in enhancing network performance, reducing latency, and improving energy efficiency. Despite these advantages, UAV communication networks face several critical challenges. These include limited battery life and energy constraints, interference management, spectrum allocation, secure communication, scalability in multi-UAV systems, and maintaining reliable connectivity in highly dynamic environments. Additionally, efficient coordination among multiple UAVs (swarm intelligence), collision avoidance, and real-time decision-making remain open research problems. To address these challenges, current research focuses on developing robust, scalable, and energy-efficient frameworks. This includes optimization-based models, graph-theoretic approaches, distributed control mechanisms, and AI-enabled adaptive systems. The ultimate goal is to design UAV-assisted networks that can provide seamless, reliable, and high-quality communication services, especially in scenarios where conventional infrastructure is unavailable or insufficient.

Information Retrieval

Information Retrieval (IR) focuses on designing efficient techniques and systems to search, extract, and organize relevant information from large-scale and heterogeneous data sources such as web documents, digital libraries, databases, and multimedia repositories. With the exponential growth of data in the digital era, IR has become a fundamental component in enabling fast, accurate, and scalable access to information across various domains. Traditional IR systems rely on keyword-based matching techniques, such as vector space models and probabilistic models (e.g., BM25), to retrieve relevant documents based on user queries. However, modern IR systems go beyond simple keyword matching by incorporating semantic understanding, context awareness, and user intent modeling. This shift has led to the development of advanced techniques such as semantic search, knowledge graphs, and neural information retrieval models. Research in this domain includes several key areas, such as efficient indexing and retrieval algorithms, ranking and relevance optimization, query expansion and reformulation, and personalized recommendation systems. Learning-to-rank approaches and deep learning models are widely used to improve ranking accuracy by capturing complex relationships between queries and documents. Additionally, Natural Language Processing (NLP) techniques play a crucial role in understanding user queries, extracting meaningful features, and enhancing retrieval quality. Recent advancements in IR are driven by the integration of machine learning and deep neural networks, including transformer-based architectures. These models enable context-aware retrieval, cross-lingual search, and conversational search systems, making IR more interactive and user-centric. Applications of IR span across web search engines, e-commerce platforms, question-answering systems, digital assistants, and enterprise knowledge management systems. Despite significant progress, several challenges remain, including handling ambiguous queries, ensuring fairness and bias mitigation, maintaining privacy, and scaling systems for real-time retrieval over massive datasets. Current research focuses on developing intelligent, adaptive, and explainable IR systems that can provide highly relevant, personalized, and trustworthy information to users in diverse and dynamic environments.

Artificial Intelligence

Artificial Intelligence (AI) has emerged as a transformative technology that enables machines to learn from data, identify patterns, and make intelligent decisions with minimal human intervention. With advancements in machine learning, deep learning, and data analytics, AI is now widely applied across various domains, including healthcare, communication systems, finance, and smart environments. Its ability to process large-scale data and extract meaningful insights has made it a powerful tool for solving complex real-world problems. In the healthcare domain, AI plays a crucial role in improving diagnosis, treatment planning, and patient monitoring. By analyzing medical data such as images, signals, and patient records, AI can assist in early detection of diseases, prediction of health conditions, and personalized treatment recommendations. It is also widely used in areas such as medical imaging, drug discovery, remote patient monitoring, and mental health assessment. The integration of AI helps in reducing human error, enhancing efficiency, and enabling proactive healthcare solutions rather than reactive treatments. Building on these advancements, recent research is increasingly focusing on leveraging wearable devices for health monitoring and disease prediction. Modern smartwatches and wearable sensors continuously collect physiological data such as heart rate, activity levels, sleep patterns, and other vital signals. By applying AI and machine learning techniques to this data, it becomes possible to identify patterns related to various health conditions, including emotional and mental states. In this direction, current work explores the potential of commercial wearable devices to predict different health-related conditions using real-world data. By utilizing large-scale datasets and advanced learning models, this approach aims to enable early detection, continuous monitoring, and more accessible healthcare solutions, highlighting the growing importance of AI-driven wearable technologies in modern healthcare systems.

Social Networks

Social Network Analysis (SNA) studies the structure, dynamics, and behavior of interconnected systems, where entities (nodes) such as individuals, organizations, or devices are linked through relationships (edges). These networks can represent online social media platforms, communication systems, biological networks, or real-world human interactions. By modeling such systems as graphs, SNA provides powerful tools to analyze how information, influence, and behaviors propagate across networks. A key focus of SNA is understanding network topology and structural properties, including centrality measures (degree, betweenness, closeness), clustering coefficients, and network density. These metrics help identify important nodes, influential users, and tightly connected communities. Community detection algorithms are widely used to uncover hidden group structures within networks, enabling better understanding of social dynamics and interaction patterns. Another major research direction is information diffusion and influence propagation, which studies how ideas, trends, or misinformation spread across a network. Models such as Independent Cascade and Linear Threshold are commonly used to simulate and analyze diffusion processes. This is particularly important in applications like viral marketing, rumor detection, and public awareness campaigns. SNA also plays a critical role in user behavior analysis, where patterns of interaction, engagement, and activity are examined to understand preferences and predict future actions. Techniques from machine learning and data mining are integrated to detect anomalies, identify fake accounts, and combat misinformation or malicious behavior in online platforms. Recent advancements in this field are driven by the integration of graph theory, machine learning, and deep learning, particularly Graph Neural Networks (GNNs). These models enable learning complex representations of nodes and edges, improving tasks such as link prediction, node classification, and recommendation systems. Additionally, temporal and dynamic network analysis has gained importance, as real-world networks continuously evolve over time. Despite its wide applicability, SNA faces several challenges, including scalability for large-scale networks, data sparsity, privacy concerns, and bias in network data. Current research focuses on developing scalable algorithms, privacy-preserving techniques, and explainable models to ensure ethical and reliable analysis. Social Network Analysis has diverse applications across domains such as marketing and advertising, public opinion analysis, recommendation systems, cybersecurity, healthcare (e.g., epidemic spread modeling), and organizational behavior analysis. Its ability to uncover hidden patterns and relationships makes it a vital tool in understanding complex interconnected systems in today’s data-driven world.
